# Executive summary template

**Purpose.** A two-page distilled view of a completed innovation project at the TRL 4 exit gate. Produced by Phase 5 as a derived artifact from the Innovation Canvas Document (ICD). Audience: internal leadership and external stakeholders (investors, partners, funders, review committees). Self-contained: a reader with no access to the ICD must be able to understand the project, the evidence, and the recommendation.

**When to produce.** For every Phase 5 decision outcome: Go, Kill, or Pivot. Kill and Pivot summaries are first-class deliverables, not consolation documents. "Why we killed it" has real decision value and honors the open-science stance of the framework.

**Length target.** Two pages maximum. If it does not fit in two pages, it is not an executive summary. Cut evidence, not structure.

**Style rules.** English or the team's working language. Sentence case throughout. No emojis. No em-dashes with spaces. Numbered lists only (no bullets). No insider jargon, no unexplained acronyms. Cite ICD section numbers in parentheses where sources matter. Obey the global style conventions in CLAUDE.md.

**Relationship to the ICD.** This document is a view, not a replacement. The ICD remains the authoritative source of record. If the executive summary and the ICD diverge, the ICD wins and the summary must be updated. Do not duplicate content verbatim. Distill.

## 8. Recommendation

## Authoring notes (remove before saving)

1. Draft in order: pitch, problem, solution, evidence, then the rest. If the first four sections are not crisp, the remaining sections will not save the document.
2. Write the Kill and Pivot variants with the same care as the Go variant. Negative results are evidence, not failure.
3. Every number cited in the executive summary must be traceable to the ICD. Do not round aggressively. Do not invent confidence levels.
4. If a reader cannot understand the project without the ICD, the summary has failed its purpose. Test this: give the draft to someone who has not seen the ICD and ask them to explain the project back.
